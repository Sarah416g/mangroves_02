Abstract

A mangrove is a salt tolerant tree that reside on coastlines within tropic or subtropic lattitudes. Mangroves serve several purposes not only provide protection for coastal communities but they encourage biodiversity, aid in reducing the amount of green house gesses in the atmosphere, as well as provide food security for many vurnurable areas. They are so unique because they not only survive but thrive in such harsh conditions.
